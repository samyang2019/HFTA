1.1 The Second Law · The Fate of Closed Systems

The second law of thermodynamics says: in an isolated system, total entropy can only increase or stay the same; it can never decrease. Entropy is a measure of disorder — the more microstates a system has, the higher its entropy. A cup of hot water in a cold room cools down on its own, but a cup of cold water never heats itself up; a broken cup never reassembles itself; an aging body never gets younger on its own. This is not coincidence; it is the deepest physical law of the universe.

F2 · Second Law of Thermodynamics · Entropy Increase in a Closed System
$$\frac{dS_{\text{closed}}}{dt} \geq 0$$
Here $S$ is entropy (the logarithmic measure of the number of microstates of the system), and $t$ is time. In an isolated system, the total rate of change of entropy is non-negative — meaning the disorder of a closed system can only increase or stay the same.

This law is the most irreversible law in the universe. It gives time its direction — the "arrow of time" is the direction of entropy increase. The final fate of a closed system is "heat death" — all energy evenly distributed, all structures dissolved, all differences erased. This is death at the cosmic scale.

1.2 Life as an Open-System Anti-Entropy Rebellion

But does life violate this law? No. Life is not a closed system; it is an open system. F3 · Entropy Equation for an Open Living System
$$\frac{dS_{\text{self}}}{dt} = \dot{S}_{\text{in}} + \dot{S}_{\text{gen}} - \dot{S}_{\text{export}}$$
$\frac{dS_{\text{self}}}{dt}$ is the local rate of entropy change of the individual; $\dot{S}_{\text{in}}$ is the rate at which low entropy is imported from outside (sleep / reading / dialogue / exercise / meditation / art / review); $\dot{S}_{\text{gen}}$ is the rate at which entropy is generated internally (decisions / emotions / metabolic waste); $\dot{S}_{\text{export}}$ is the rate at which entropy is exported to the outside (writing / exercise / creation / boundary-setting).

Life can maintain its internal order if and only if $\dot{S}_{\text{in}} + \dot{S}_{\text{export}} > \dot{S}_{\text{gen}}$ — that is, the low entropy drawn from outside plus the high entropy exported to outside must exceed the entropy generated internally. This is the physical definition of life.

2.6 The Dopamine System · The Signal Is Not the Master

The value-over-dopamine theory states: dopamine is not the "pleasure molecule," it is the "wanting" neurotransmitter. It is driven by the pathway projecting from the ventral tegmental area (VTA) of the midbrain to the nucleus accumbens, and it measures the "reward prediction error" (RPE) — the difference between actual reward and expected reward.

F12 · Dopamine Reward Prediction Error (RPE)
$$\delta_t = R_t - \mathbb{E}[R_t \mid s_t] = R_t - V(s_t)$$
$\delta_t$ is the reward prediction error at time $t$ (the dopamine signal); $R_t$ is the actual reward received; $\mathbb{E}[R_t \mid s_t]$ is the expected reward given state $s_t$, denoted $V(s_t)$. 1.E.1 Twelve Axioms of Entropy-Reduction Ontology

  1. Open-System Axiom — A human being is not a closed system. To maintain order, a person must take in energy, information, relationships, tools, and meaning. The key to life, therefore, is not "refusing all input" but establishing boundaries for input.
  2. Energy-First Axiom — Any higher-order order depends on lower-order energy. When bodily energy is insufficient, will, reason, aesthetics, moral judgment, and long-termism all degrade significantly.
  3. Attention-Shaping Axiom — Where attention consistently flows, there neural connections, emotional patterns, action habits, and the sense of identity reshape themselves. Attention carries an inescapable moral weight.
  4. Boundary-Generates-Order Axiom — Without boundaries there is no structure; without structure there is no accumulation. Boundaries do not close life off; they keep life from being swallowed by randomness.
  5. Structure-Compresses-Complexity Axiom — Every effective method is, in essence, a compression of complex reality into structures that are understandable, decidable, executable, and reusable.
  6. Feedback-Selects-Reality Axiom — Systems without feedback become addicted to self-explanation; systems with feedback gradually approach reality. The shorter, more accurate, and more actionable the feedback, the higher the rate of entropy reduction.
  7. Closure-Settles-Debt Axiom — Every unfinished, unexplained, unfiled, unanswered, unrepaired item becomes system debt and collects interest in the form of anxiety, rework, misunderstanding, or lost opportunity.
  8. Few-High-Leverage Axiom — Most of life's outcomes are determined by a handful of core variables. Extreme entropy reduction is not managing everything but identifying and controlling the dominant variables.
  9. Identity-Consistency Axiom — When a person's values, narratives, habits, work, and relationships conflict with each other, existential entropy rises; when they reinforce each other, the cost of action falls.
  10. Relational-Field Axiom — Human beings live in a relational field. High-trust relationships lower transaction costs; low-trust relationships generate constant noise. Relational quality is part of a person's entropy-reduction capacity.
  11. Repair-Over-Perfection Axiom — Every system drifts. True high-order order is not never drifting but having the capacity to detect, admit, repair, review, and redesign.
  12. Creative-Outlet Axiom — If the order of life only contracts inward, it ossifies; if it can create value outward, order gains a reason to keep renewing itself.

3.E.5 Iterative-Refinement Theorem · The 80% Closure Threshold

Thinking precision rises with the number of "hypothesis — test — revise" iterations, following a diminishing-returns curve: the first 3–5 iterations typically reach 80% precision, after which marginal returns drop sharply.

$$\text{Precision}(n) = 1 - e^{-\lambda n}, \quad \text{Precision}(3\text{-}5) \approx 0.8$$
$\text{Precision}(n)$ is the precision after $n$ iterations; $\lambda$ is the convergence rate; $e^{-\lambda n}$ is the residual uncertainty (exponentially decaying). After 3–5 iterations precision reaches 80%, and marginal returns drop sharply thereafter. Operational corollary: actively close at 80% precision and deploy the remaining resources to a new problem.

Value Over Dopamine · The Sovereignty Engineering of the Reward System

The enemy of compounding and the open world is not "lack of willpower" but the hijacking of the dopamine system. This section integrates the "value over dopamine" theory, revealing how the reward system is hijacked and how it can be reclaimed.

8.E.1 Dopamine-Hijacking Theorem

When a dopamine pathway is repeatedly reinforced by artificial stimulation, it takes over the inhibitory function of the prefrontal cortex. Hijacking unfolds in four stages: contact → habituation → tolerance → hijacking. Early intervention is far more effective than late intervention.

F-E5 · Dopamine-Hijacking Theorem
$$\frac{dQ(s, a)}{dt} = \alpha \cdot \delta(t) - \beta \cdot Q(s, a) \cdot \mathbb{1}_{PFC\_inhibited}$$
$Q(s, a)$ is the value estimate of action $a$ in state $s$ (the "habit strength" learned by the dopamine pathway); $\alpha$ is the learning rate; $\delta(t)$ is the reward-prediction error (F12); $\beta$ is the inhibitory strength of the prefrontal cortex; $\mathbb{1}_{PFC\_inhibited}$ is the indicator function for PFC inhibition (0 or 1). When the PFC is inhibited (fatigue / emotional arousal / alcohol), $\beta \cdot Q \cdot \mathbb{1} = 0$, and the habit $Q$ grows without inhibition — this is the neuroscience of "I can't control myself."

8.E.2 Value-Delay Theorem · The Structural Contradiction Between Pleasure and Value

"Pleasure" and "valuable" are structurally contradictory — the more valuable the activity, the more unpleasant its early phase. This is because the dopamine system was designed for immediate rewards (the Pleistocene environment), whereas modern valuable activities (deep work / learning / creation / relationships) are mostly delayed-reward.

F-E6 · Value-Delay Theorem
$$\Delta V(a) \propto \int_0^T e^{-r\tau} \cdot \mathcal{O}(s_\tau, a) \, d\tau \quad \text{while} \quad \delta_{\text{dopamine}}(t) \approx 0 \quad \text{when} \quad t \ll T$$
$\Delta V(a)$ is the value increment of action $a$; $\int_0^T e^{-r\tau} \mathcal{O} \, d\tau$ is the exponentially discounted integral of the future order increment $\mathcal{O}$ ($r$ is the discount rate); $\delta_{\text{dopamine}}(t)$ is the dopamine signal at moment $t$; $T$ is the time point at which value is realized. When $t \ll T$ (the early phase), dopamine $\approx 0$ (no immediate reward), but value is accumulating — this is the mathematics of "unpleasant early, valuable long-term."

8.E.3 Compounding-Nonlinearity Theorem · The Valley of Despair and the Peak of Miracles

F-E7 · Compounding-Nonlinearity Theorem
$$\frac{K(T)}{K_0} = e^{rT} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \begin{cases} T = 1/r: & 2.7 \times \\ T = 5/r: & 148 \times \\ T = 10/r: & 22026 \times \end{cases}$$
$K(T)/K_0$ is the multiple of the initial asset at time $T$; $r$ is the compounding rate. At $T = 1/r$, the asset grows only 2.7× (the valley of despair); at $T = 5/r$, it grows 148× (the miracle begins); at $T = 10/r$, it grows 22,026× (the peak of miracles). Counter-intuitive corollary: the compounding curve looks "useless" when $t < 5/r$ and suddenly explodes when $t > 5/r$ — this is the norm, not the exception.

$$T_e^{\text{net}} = T_{\text{raw}} - n \cdot C_{\text{switch}} - S_{\text{leak}} \cdot n$$

8.E.5 Threshold-Drift Theorem · The Stronger the Stimulus, the Higher the Threshold

F-E9 · Threshold-Drift Theorem
$$\theta_{t+1} = \theta_t + \eta \cdot \max(0, \delta_t - \theta_t), \quad \eta > 0$$
$\theta_t$ is the dopamine threshold at moment $t$; $\eta$ is the drift learning rate; $\delta_t$ is the reward-prediction error (F12); $\max(0, \delta_t - \theta_t)$ means the threshold drifts only when $\delta_t$ exceeds the current threshold. Key point: high-intensity stimuli push $\delta_t$ far above $\theta_t$, raising the threshold in a way that is short-term irreversible. About 30 days in a low-stimulation environment are needed for $\theta$ to return to normal.

This theorem explains "why the more short videos you scroll, the more bored you feel" — every video sends $\delta_t$ above $\theta_t$, $\theta$ rises, and the next time you need an even stronger stimulus to trigger the same $\delta$. The result: the stimulus intensity of ordinary life (walking / reading / conversation) falls below the drifted $\theta$, fails to trigger dopamine, and so feels "boring". The remedy is 30 days in a low-stimulation environment — letting $\theta$ naturally decline.

$$Q_{\text{new}}(s, a) = Q_{\text{old}}(s, a) + \alpha \sum_{k=1}^{N} \delta_{\text{marked}}^{(k)}$$
